An average rep (7 boats/year) sits down with Robinson: "Give me better leads and I'll close more. I work just as hard as anyone."
Is she right about lead quality — or is it a skill gap? Work through the tasks below, then two members act out the first 60 seconds of the meeting.

Argument: It's Lead Quality

  • If top reps qualify differently, Robinson should fix it upstream
  • Better prospect screening would lift everyone's numbers
Argument: It's a Skill Gap
Same leads. Very different results.
  • All reps pull from the same pool — 20 leads/month each
  • Top reps meet on model boats; average reps use office
  • Prospect → 2nd meeting: 45% vs 30% on identical leads

The Final Question
Robinson walks into the CEO's office Monday morning. He can only make one recommendation. What is it?
This is an individual vote — not a group decision. Every student picks independently before any discussion happens. Pick the option you genuinely believe is the highest-leverage move. You cannot say "all of the above." Your instructor will reveal the distribution and call on people from the minority opinion to defend their pick.
A
Overhaul the media mix — cut print, shift to digital. Stop paying $250K per sale for regional magazines.
B
Narrow to the entrepreneur profile and rewrite the message. Better targeting beats more spending.
C

D
Hold the line — in economic uncertainty, the worst move is a major change. Protect what's working.
