The Situation
An average rep (7 boats/year) sits down with Robinson: "Give me better leads and I'll close more. I work just as hard as anyone."
Is she right about lead quality — or is it a skill gap? Work through the tasks below, then two members act out the first 60 seconds of the meeting.

MB vs Competitor Referrals3% vs 9%
Argument: It's Lead Quality
The pipeline is the problem
  • All reps get ~20 leads/month from the same funnel
  • If top reps qualify differently, Robinson should fix it upstream
  • Better prospect screening would lift everyone's numbers
Argument: It's a Skill Gap
Same leads. Very different results.
  • All reps pull from the same pool — 20 leads/month each
  • Top reps meet on model boats; average reps use office
  • Prospect → 2nd meeting: 45% vs 30% on identical leads
A
Lead quality or skill gap — pick one
Take a position. Use the data. You cannot say "both."
B
One specific, observable change she can make
Concrete and testable in 60 days. How would Robinson measure it?
C
Should leads be redistributed?
Top reps more, average reps fewer. Upside? Risk? Try to quantify.
D
Write a one-sentence referral policy
MB gets 3%; boutique competitors get 9%. Specific trigger + specific incentive. Class votes on the best.
